10 October is World Mental Health Day

The overall objective of World Mental Health Day is to raise awareness of mental health issues around the world and to mobilize efforts in support of mental health. The Day provides an opportunity for all stake holders working on mental health issues to talk about their work, and what more needs to be done to make mental health care a reality for people worldwide.







What is Mental Health ?

Mental health includes our emotional, social, and psychological well-being. It affects how we think and act and how we interact with others. It encompasses a wide range of aspects related to how individuals think, feel, and act. Good mental health is not just the absence of mental health disorders; it involves the presence of positive attributes and the ability to cope with life's challenges effectively.





Why it is important ?

mental health is vital for students because it influences their academic success, emotional well-being, physical health, social relationships, personal development, resilience, and long-term well-being. Promoting mental health in educational settings is essential for ensuring that students have the support and resources they need to thrive academically and personally.

What is Stress ?

Stress can be defined as a state of worry or mental tension caused by a difficult situation. Stress is a natural human response that prompts us to address challenges and threats in our lives.

What causes stress ?

Factors such as homework, social life, perceived parental pressure, university applications, and never-ending workloads all generate stress. Although research shows that a moderate amount of stress can be beneficial and act as a motivator for students to do well, too much stress can impact their overall well-being.

Physical symptoms of stress

People react differently to stress. Some common symptoms of stress include sleeping problems, sweating, or a change in appetite.

You may experience headaches, muscle tension, pain, nausea, indigestion and dizziness. You may also breathe more quickly, have palpitations or suffer from various aches and pains. In the long term, you may be putting yourself at risk of heart attacks and strokes.

Emotional effects of stress

When you are stressed you may have lots of different feelings, including anxiety, irritability or low self-esteem, which can lead you to become withdrawn, indecisive or tearful.

You may have periods of constant worry, racing thoughts, or repeatedly going over the same things in your head. Some people experience changes in their behaviour. They may lose their temper more easily, act irrationally or become more verbally or physically aggressive. These feelings can feed on each other and produce physical symptoms, which can make you feel even worse. For example, extreme anxiety can make you feel so unwell that you then worry you have a serious physical condition.

Benefits

Improved physical health

Life satisfaction
